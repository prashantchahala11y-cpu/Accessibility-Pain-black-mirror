The real horror?

These are not rare edge cases. These issues appear on real websites, real apps, and real user journeys.

For some users, an inaccessible website does not mean inconvenience. It means dependency, delay, exclusion, and loss of independence.

What clients should remember

  • A beautiful UI is not successful if users cannot complete the task.
  • Accessibility issues are not just technical defects; they create real human barriers.
  • Most of these problems are preventable when accessibility is considered from design and development stages.
  • Testing only with a mouse and perfect vision does not represent all users.
  • Inclusive products reduce risk, improve usability, and build trust.
If people cannot use it, it is not good design.
